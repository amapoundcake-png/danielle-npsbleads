About

Danni Adams walks into rooms she was never supposed to enter. She has spoken at Harvard University, appeared in Vogue, and built a community of over 52,000 people who trust her voice above the noise. She does not do inspiration. She does accountability, and every audience she has addressed has left ready to think differently.

She is the Co-Creator of the Institute for Body Image, a professional development program training medical providers in inclusive, body-positive care. As a recurring guest on national television, including the Tamron Hall Show, Fox News, and TLC, she has proven that the conversations she starts on stage have consequences that extend well beyond the room.

She has spoken at Harvard University, the University of Ottawa, Full Sail University, Bethune-Cookman University, and the Seminole Leadership Conference, and was recognized as a Nonprofit Leader Award recipient for her impact in Central Florida. Whether she is in a lecture hall, on a panel, or in front of a camera, she does not let audiences leave comfortable.

She does not ask for a seat at the table. She questions who built it and who keeps getting left standing.
